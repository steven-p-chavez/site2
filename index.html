Steven Paul Chavez

My Ph.D. research involves three topics: 1. Characterization of the blind zone and orographic precipitation in the Andes using the GPM precipitation radar and in-situ disdrometer and radar measurements. 2. The Stutdy of aerosol Indirect Efects on Orographic Clouds and Precipitation under diferent weather regimes using the WRF model and evaluation with aircraft observations. 3. Modeling aerosol-cloud interactions by coupling a bin microphysics scheme with an LES model.

Objective: Characterize the microphysics of orographic clouds and precipitation in mountainous regions

1) Investigate the physical basis of error in remote sensing of orographic precipitation in the Andes.

2) How aerosol-cloud-precipitation interactions modulate orographic precipitation.         

  • 1.1 Dominat Microphysical Processes
    Precipitation is mainly stratiform in the Central Andes of Peru. The dominant microphysical processes are aggregation, melting and breakup.
  • 1.2 GPM DPR Blind zone
    The GPM DPR blind zone misses 47% of liquid precipitation and the variability of the rain drop size distribution at the ground
  • 1.3 Modeling of microphysical processes
    Explicity microphysical modeling of the blind zone is not possible because ice processes require NWP to represent the full lifecycle of the precipitating systems.
  • 2.1 Under what conditions do Aerosol-cloud interactions govern rainfall onset?
    Under weak synoptic conditions ACI impact the onset of precipitation.
  • 2.2 What about aerosol-cloud interactions in strong synoptic conditions?
    Enhanced moisture convergence results in high rainfall efficiency overcoming the Aerosol Indirect Effect (AIE).
  • 2.3 Using a regional aerosol activation spectrum in WRF is sufficient to capture the cloud structure?
    The regional aerosol captures better than Continental the variability near cloud base but is not sufficient because it doesn’t capture the full vertical cloud structure compared to aircraft observations.
