Cloud modeling is a multi-scale challenge

Aerosol radius except for giant aerosol are mainly in the orden of 10-9 to 10-7 m. Rain droplets are in the order of 10-3 m. And cloud droplets are in the middle , i.e.,   10-6 to 10-4 m .     Building a model that includes    all those processes is a monumental project. A two step approach is commonly used to modelate precipitating clouds,     first assume cloud droplets distributions  and  then focusing on the growing of precipitating particles. However, in the case of warm clouds and rain   its possible   to solve for both clouds and precipitation using bin microphysics and a generalization of the collision processes without breakout.            

  • Aerosol Chemistry
    Aerosols properties such as: Hygroscopicity, radius, number concentration, etc. Are the input of the model.
  • Atmospheric Conditions
    A vertical profile of pressure, potential temperature, especific humidity, zonal   wind velocity  is used and its assumed homogeneity in the horizontal dimension
  • Cloud properties
    Bin microphysics will produce a distribution of drop sizes and number concentrations. From there cloud properties like liquid water conten, reflectivity and cloud optical depth are calculated.
